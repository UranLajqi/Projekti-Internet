What Job Can You Get With a BS in Computer Engineering?

  • Hardware Engineering
  • Software Development
  • Database Administration
  • Information Security

Hardware Engineering

If you opt to become a computer hardware engineer, your job could involve designing, testing or analyzing computer hardware, or overseeing the manufacturing process of computers and computerized equipment. Today, computer hardware isn't limited to laptops, desktops and servers. You could be working on any device that has a computer chip in it, or is internet accessible, including new phones, cars, medical devices, and home appliances like smart fridges or washing machines. Most of these positions are with the companies that manufacture the devices. In 2017, there were about 73,600 computer hardware engineering positions in the United States, with a median income of $115,120. The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics estimated job growth to be about five percent between 2016 and 2026, which is about average.

Software Development

If you choose to go the software route, you'll find there are more than 16 times as many jobs to choose from, compared to jobs in computer hardware. If this ratio doesn't make sense, take a look at your smartphone. For every team of engineers who worked on the hardware, there was another team working on the phone's software, plus another team of developers for each of the thousands of apps available from the phone's app store. Most jobs in software are in software development, although some positions are still known as computer programming. If you work in software, you can work with hardware engineers in developing the operating system and the drivers used to access other devices, such as screens, printers and Wi-Fi access points. A large number of positions involve creating the apps. These jobs typically include testing and updating the software to keep them current and secure. In 2016, the BLS estimated there were over 1.2 million software development positions in the United States, with an estimated growth of 24 percent over the next ten years, which is much higher than average. The 2017 median income for these jobs was $103,560 per year.

Database Administration

Database administrators are software specialists who work with programs designed to organize and store data, like the financial records at a bank, or the inventory and customer information for a retailer. DBAs are responsible for keeping the information accessible to those who need it, while keeping it inaccessible to those who shouldn't have access to it. The median income for database administrators in 2017 was $87,020. The BLS estimated there were 119,500 positions in 2016, with a ten-year projected growth rate of 11 percent.
